    <h1 id="home-about-title">About</h1>
    <p>Hi! I'm Chenlu Wang, originally from Xi'an, China, a city known as the capital of thirteen dynasties and, just as memorably, for culinary icons such as Roujiamo, Liangpi, and Yangrou Paomo.</p>
    <p>I earned my Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering (2024) from the Institute of Process Engineering (IPE), Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS). My work focuses on computational materials chemistry, where I combine density functional theory (DFT), molecular dynamics (MD), and force field development to understand complex molecular and ionic systems.</p>
    <p>My current interests include electrolytes, ionic liquids, nanoconfined transport, and interfaces involving 1D and 2D materials. I aim to uncover molecular-level mechanisms of structure, interaction, transport, and thermodynamics, with applications in electrochemical energy storage, CO₂ separation, and smart functional materials.</p>
    <p>Outside the lab, my cat clearly believes she runs the household. I'm also a Les Mills instructor, which means I spend a fair amount of time helping people move, sweat, and leave feeling stronger. I also enjoy cooking, watching films, reading, and traveling.</p>
    <p>If you're interested in molecular simulations, fitness, or just trading cat photos, <a href="mailto:{{ site.author.email }}">let's connect!</a></p>
