Welcome to my personal website.

Hi there! This is Chenlu Wang. Thanks for visiting my personal website.

I'm from Jiangsu, China, and currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at Suzhou Laboratory. I received my Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS). My research mainly focuses on computational materials chemistry, molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, density functional theory (DFT) calculations, and polarizable force field development for complex molecular and ionic systems.

My current interests center on ionic liquids, battery electrolytes, nanoconfined fluids, and interfaces involving 2D materials. By combining MD simulations, Drude polarizable models, machine-learning potentials, and DFT calculations, I explore molecular structures, interfacial interactions, and transport mechanisms in ionic and hybrid materials. My goal is to better understand and design materials for energy storage, CO₂ separation, and smart functional systems.

If you are interested in molecular simulations, DFT, polarizable force fields, or machine learning potentials, feel free to reach out - I'd love to exchange ideas and learn together!

Outside the lab, I spend time with my cat, enjoy working out, watching movies, reading, and traveling. I believe that interdisciplinary research is the key to pushing the boundaries of materials science, and I'm always open to collaboration!
