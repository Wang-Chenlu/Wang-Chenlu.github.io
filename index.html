About

Hi! I'm Chenlu Wang, originally from Xi'an, China, a city known as the capital of thirteen dynasties and, just as memorably, for culinary icons such as Roujiamo, Liangpi, and Yangrou Paomo.

I earned my Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering (2024) from the Institute of Process Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences. My work focuses on computational materials chemistry, where I combine density functional theory (DFT), molecular dynamics (MD), and force field development to understand complex molecular and ionic systems.

My current interests include electrolytes, ionic liquids, nanoconfined transport, and interfaces involving 1D and 2D materials. I aim to uncover molecular-level mechanisms of structure, interaction, transport, and thermodynamics, with applications in electrochemical energy storage, CO₂ separation, and smart functional materials.

Outside the lab, my cat clearly believes she runs the household. I'm also a Les Mills instructor, which means I spend a fair amount of time helping people move, sweat, and leave feeling stronger. I also enjoy cooking, watching films, reading, and traveling.

If you're interested in molecular simulations, fitness, or just trading cat photos, let's connect!
